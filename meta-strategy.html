The Sign-Up Happens Where Meta Cannot See It

This is the single most important thing to fix before a penny is spent. Right now the conversion is invisible to the pixel.

Why this matters

A pixel only fires on the domain where its code is installed. Because the sign-up is completed on Ashbourne, a domain BasicGym does not control, Meta currently records zero completed sign-ups. With no conversion signal, the algorithm cannot optimise toward real members, reporting cannot be trusted, and budget cannot be scaled with confidence. Fixing this is step one.

Riaz's instinct is correct

The proposal to redirect a completed sign-up back to a thank-you page on the BasicGym site is the right fix, and it is the most reliable option available without code access to Ashbourne. The next slide sets out how we make it work.

The Fix

A Thank-You Page That Catches the Conversion

We capture two signals: the click into the sign-up, and the completed sign-up returning to a page we control.

Signal 1

Join is clicked

Fire an InitiateCheckout event on the Join button. This gives us a high volume early signal and builds an audience of people who started but did not finish.

On the BasicGym site
→
Ashbourne

Member signs up

The visitor completes the three steps inside the Ashbourne system as they do today.

→
Signal 2

Redirect to /welcome

On completion, Ashbourne sends the member to a new thank-you page on basicgym.co.uk. That page fires the Purchase event through both the pixel and the Conversions API.

The conversion we optimise to

What this depends on

  • Ashbourne must support a success redirect URL. We confirm this on day one. Most management platforms allow a configurable return or completion URL.
  • A thank-you page is built at a fixed address such as basicgym.co.uk/welcome. The web company can do this inside their existing retainer.
  • A value, and ideally a reference, is passed back. If Ashbourne can append the sign-up value or a transaction ID to the redirect, tracking and de-duplication get stronger.
  • basicgym.co.uk is verified in Meta Business Manager so the conversion is trusted.

The honest caveat

The thank-you page only fires if Ashbourne actually redirects on completion. A member who closes the tab on the final Ashbourne screen will not trigger it. We expect this to capture the large majority of sign-ups, not every single one.

The safety net

Every week we reconcile Meta against the actual sign-up export from the gym system. The gym system is the source of truth for member numbers. Meta is the source of truth for what drove them. We report both.

Measurement

The Full Tracking Setup

Clean signal in means clean optimisation out. This is the foundation every other decision rests on.

EventWhere it firesPurposePriority
PageViewEvery page on basicgym.co.ukBase signal, already in place, to be verifiedConfirm
ViewContentHomepage and offer sectionInterest signal and retargeting poolBuild
InitiateCheckoutClick on the Join buttonHigh volume early optimisation event and abandoner audienceKey
PurchaseThe new /welcome thank-you pageThe completed sign-up. The event we scale towardCritical

Conversions API

Server side tracking runs alongside the browser pixel on the thank-you page. It recovers conversions lost to ad blockers and mobile privacy limits, which matters more than usual because this conversion fires after a cross-domain journey on mobile. We de-duplicate using a shared event ID.

Which event we optimise to

We launch optimising for the completed sign-up where early volume allows. If the new pixel is short on Purchase data in the first days, we optimise to InitiateCheckout to feed the algorithm, then switch to Purchase once volume supports it. Meta needs roughly 50 events a week to stabilise.

How we verify it

Before launch every event is checked in Events Manager Test Events and the Meta Pixel Helper browser tool, with Diagnostics monitored after. Nothing goes live on guesswork. We confirm each event fires once, with the right value, on the right action.

One reporting note

In January 2026 Meta retired the longer view-through attribution windows. We report on the current default windows and reconcile against the gym system weekly, so the numbers you see in Meta line up with the members you see in the building.

Almost all paid traffic arrives on mobile. A slow, heavy page loses a share of those clicks before anyone sees the Join button. This section runs in every review from here on.

Fixes, in priority order

  • Compress and resize images. The page payload is the biggest single problem. Properly sized, modern format, lazy loaded images will cut load time sharply.
  • Stop the layout jumping. A shift score of 1.348 means the page moves around as it loads, causing mis-taps and exits. Setting fixed dimensions on images and media fixes most of this.
  • Trim unused code. The report flags roughly 588 KB of unused JavaScript and 40 KB of unused CSS, plus render blocking requests to defer.
  • Set cache lifetimes so repeat visitors load faster.

Source: Google PageSpeed Insights, mobile, 8 June 2026. We brief these to the web company and re-test after.

The sign-up form is heavy for a £5 offer

The Ashbourne flow asks for personal details, contact details, a full address and bank details across three steps. That is a lot of friction for a five pound decision, so drop-off will be real.

  • Set expectations before the handoff. A short line such as "takes two minutes, have your bank details handy" reduces surprise exits.
  • Keep the BasicGym page focused. One offer, one clear Join, social proof from Long Eaton, a countdown to opening.
  • Retarget the people who start but do not finish. This is one of our biggest levers and is covered in audiences.
  • Track the completion rate from Join click to finished sign-up as a core funnel health metric.

Campaign Build

Account Structure

The rule that matters most in 2026 is consolidation. We keep the signal concentrated so Meta's algorithm can learn fast, rather than splitting budget across a dozen small ad sets.

How we build it

  • One campaign, one objective. A Sales campaign optimising for the completed sign-up. Mixing objectives fragments the conversion signal.
  • Advantage+ Sales with a broad, AI-driven audience. Granular interest stacking is no longer the way to win. We give Meta room to find members.
  • One to three ad sets during learning. Each ad set is a separate learning event, so we keep them few and well fed.
  • A hybrid approach, not blind automation. We pair Meta's automation with our own creative direction, exclusions and guardrails rather than handing over the whole account cold.
  • Existing Long Eaton members excluded from prospecting so budget chases new members, not people already paying us.

The rules we hold to

Respect the learning phase

Meta needs roughly 50 optimisation events per week per ad set to stabilise. During learning, costs run 20 to 50 percent higher and swing around. We do not panic in week one.

Do not reset the algorithm

Significant edits restart learning. We make changes deliberately, not daily, and let winning structures run.

Scale in steps

Budget increases of 10 to 20 percent at a time, with a few days between moves. Doubling overnight shocks delivery and spikes cost.

Targeting

Geography and Audiences

A gym is a local business. People travel roughly four miles for one. Geography is our sharpest lever, layered with the data BasicGym already owns.

Where we point the ads

  • A radius around Riverside Retail Park, covering the core four mile catchment first, then widening as we scale.
  • The population centres that feed the site: Nottingham, West Bridgford, Wilford, Clifton, Beeston, and the Long Eaton and Loughborough corridor.
  • The daytime workforce around Boots headquarters and the Queens Drive and Lenton Lane corridors, for pre-work, lunchtime and post-work usage.
  • Broad age and minimal filters otherwise, because the creative does the targeting now and the gym is genuinely for everyone.

The data BasicGym already owns

  • The Long Eaton member list. Uploaded with consent, this seeds a lookalike of people who already chose BasicGym, and doubles as an exclusion.
  • Social engagers. The 1,600 and growing followers, plus everyone who has watched the video content, become a warm retargeting pool.
  • Website visitors. Anyone who viewed the offer but did not click Join.
  • Abandoned sign-ups. People who clicked Join but did not finish on Ashbourne. Given the heavy form, this is a large and high-intent pool. Retargeting it is one of the strongest moves available.

The sequence

Cold prospecting carries the volume and finds new members. A retargeting layer catches the warm audience, the social engagers and above all the people who started a sign-up and stalled. As that abandoner pool builds in the first two weeks, it becomes some of the cheapest membership we can win.

Creative

Creative Is the Biggest Lever

With targeting now automated, the creative carries the result. BasicGym already has a production line and a library of angles to draw from.

The angles we already have scripts for

The recorded scripts cover a strong spread of audiences. Each becomes a distinct creative concept the algorithm can match to different people:

Beginners and starters Budget and value seekers Serious lifters Night and shift workers Class and community lovers Students Busy professionals Local to Riverside Mums

The formats we run

  • Vertical, mobile-first video with a strong hook in the first three seconds and captions on by default.
  • Build and behind-the-scenes footage. The gym taking shape, painted in the orange and black, creates anticipation and proof it is real.
  • Offer-led statics stating the £5 first month, no contract, opening 22 August.
  • Social proof. The 1,800 members and 101 five star reviews from Long Eaton answer the only question a Nottingham stranger has: is this any good.

Volume and refresh

We run 10 to 15 live creatives and add three to five fresh ones each week from the existing production line. Studies of large ad samples show only a small fraction of ads carry most of the spend, so volume is how we find the winners.

Urgency and scarcity

Founding membership framing, the price rising at opening, and a countdown to 22 August give people a reason to act now rather than later.

One brand rule we keep

No Porsche on screen. BasicGym is a low budget brand and the message stays on value, the space and the people, exactly as Riaz has set out.

Launch

Live Within 7 Days

A realistic build sequence that gets clean campaigns live inside a week, assuming access and the Ashbourne redirect are confirmed early.

Day 1

Confirm ad account and asset access. Audit the existing pixel with the Pixel Helper. Verify the domain in Business Manager. Raise the Ashbourne redirect question. Pause the wasteful post boosting.

Day 2

Brief the web company on the /welcome thank-you page and the Join-click and Purchase events. Specify the Conversions API. Request a value or reference parameter from Ashbourne.

Day 3

Quality-check every event in Test Events and Pixel Helper. Upload the Long Eaton list. Build the custom, lookalike and retargeting audiences.

Day 4

Build the campaign. Assemble the first 8 to 12 creatives from the existing library across the core angles. Write the primary copy.

Day 5

Internal quality pass on tracking, creative, targeting and budget caps. Send the budget plan and first creatives to Riaz for sign-off.

Day 6

Apply any final changes. Set up the Slack channel, the weekly reconciliation sheet and the standing weekly strategy call.

Day 7

Launch. Begin daily monitoring through the learning phase.

Quick wins running in parallel

Stop boosting posts immediately and move that money into proper campaigns. Brief the worst website speed issues to the web company. Point the organic content and ground team at the same offer and deadline.

How we decide when to scale

Green. Cost per sign-up at or below target and frequency healthy. Increase budget 10 to 20 percent.
Amber. Cost drifting up or frequency climbing. Hold budget, refresh creative.
Red. Cost well above target, or frequency high with falling click-through. Pause, restructure, new creative.

Why budget is not the constraint

Riaz has been clear that results matter more than spend. This plan is a sensible starting shape. If the cost per sign-up proves strong in the first two weeks, we have every reason to spend faster, and the budget can rise well beyond this within the stated appetite. What We Report

The Numbers That Matter

Riaz wants one weekly view built on real members, not vanity metrics. That is how we report.

Primary, the ones we are judged on

  • Completed £5 sign-ups, reconciled against the gym system. The north star.
  • Cost per sign-up. What each member costs us to acquire.
  • Weekly run-rate against target. Are we on pace for opening day.

Secondary, the ones that explain why

  • Join clicks and completion rate. How many start versus finish, the clearest read on funnel friction.
  • Click-through rate and cost per click. Creative and audience health.
  • Frequency. A tight geo fatigues fast, so we watch this closely.
  • Cost per sign-up by creative. Where the money should move next.

The weekly rhythm

A Slack channel for day-to-day questions and a single standing weekly strategy call. Every call answers the same three questions: how many members did we get, why, and what do we change to do better next week.

The reconciliation

Each week we line up Meta's reported sign-ups against the actual gym system export. The gym system is the truth on member count. Meta tells us what drove them. No gap goes unexplained.

No surprises on spend

We always agree the budget with you before we spend it, and we flag any change as results come in.
